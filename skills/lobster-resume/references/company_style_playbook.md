# Company Style Playbook

Use this only when selecting the resume tone and layout for a specific JD.

## Conservative Corporate

Use for banks, securities, insurance, consulting, accounting, law, state-owned enterprises, manufacturing headquarters, and large traditional companies.

- Layout: one page, clean grid, strong dates, restrained typography.
- Tone: precise, credible, risk-aware, business impact first.
- Emphasis: GPA/rank, brand-name institutions, awards, internships, analysis, compliance, stakeholder communication.
- Avoid: playful wording, bright colors, excessive icons, dense technical jargon without business meaning.

## Technology And Product

Use for internet companies, SaaS, AI, developer tools, data roles, product roles, and startups.

- Layout: ATS-friendly, compact sections, strong skills block, project links when available.
- Tone: direct, impact-driven, technical but readable.
- Emphasis: stack, data scale, model/product metrics, automation, experimentation, shipped features.
- Avoid: generic self-evaluation, vague teamwork claims, unsupported buzzwords.

## Creative, Media, Brand, And Consumer

Use for advertising, marketing, content, design, games, fashion, consumer brands, and entertainment.

- Layout: still ATS-safe, but may use a slightly warmer heading style and stronger portfolio/link placement.
- Tone: energetic, audience-aware, commercially literate.
- Emphasis: campaigns, user insight, content performance, visual/product taste, cross-channel execution.
- Avoid: overdesigned resume unless the user specifically asks for a visual portfolio resume.

## Research, Quant, And Academic

Use for labs, quant research, graduate applications, research internships, and deep technical roles.

- Layout: publication/project-forward, methods visible, concise research summary.
- Tone: rigorous and evidence-heavy.
- Emphasis: papers, datasets, experiments, algorithms, math/statistics, reproducibility, advisor/lab when helpful.
- Avoid: salesy claims and inflated ownership.

## International Or English JD

Use English when the JD is English or the user targets overseas roles.

- Use accomplishment verbs and concise bullets.
- Translate Chinese awards and institutions clearly; keep original names when brand recognition matters.
- Convert GPA/rank only when truthful and helpful.
- Prefer `Selected Experience`, `Projects`, `Education`, `Skills`, `Awards`.

## Style Selection Heuristic

If multiple categories match, choose the one closest to the employer's evaluation culture:

1. Regulated or prestige-screening employer -> Conservative Corporate.
2. Engineering/product/data-heavy JD -> Technology And Product.
3. Brand/content/design-growth JD -> Creative, Media, Brand, And Consumer.
4. Research-methods/publication-heavy JD -> Research, Quant, And Academic.
